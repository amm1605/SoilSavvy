    • Rice

    Aspect Description
    General Info Rice is a tropical crop that can be grown almost throughout the year. It depends on atmospheric moisture and rainfall for irrigation. India is the 2nd largest producer of rice in the world. India has the largest area in the world under rice cultivation. Productivity is low compared to wheat because the Green Revolution primarily boosted wheat production in India. The traditional rice fields are known as paddy fields and require to be flooded with 10-12 cm deep water in the early stages.
    Type of Crop Kharif, Rabi
    Temperature 20°C to 35°C during growth
    Terrain Soils with good water retention capacity with high amount of clay and organic matter are ideal for rice cultivation
    Rainfall At least 1,000 mm annually; well-distributed
    Climate Tropical and subtropical
    Soil Type Well-drained, fertile loamy soil
    Irrigation Strategy The use of best irrigation method for rice – Drip irrigation in rice combined with fertigation has a benefit for rice production
    Insecticide and Pesticide :Copper sulfate pentahydrate and diflubenzuron are narrow-spectrum pesticides that have a moderate duration impact, lasting between a few days and two weeks.
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium
    Life Cycle Germination, vegetative growth, reproductive stage, maturation
    Disease and Management Bacterial leaf blight, blast, sheath blight; crop rotation, resistant varieties, fungicides

    Wheat

    Aspect Description
    General Info Wheat is the 2nd most important food crop in India. It is a Rabi crop. India stands second in production of wheat worldwide. It is more flexible in terms of climactic and other conditions of growth.
    Type of Crop Rabi
    Temperature 17°C to 20°C during growth
    Terrain The geographical conditions needed for wheat cultivation are: A gentle slope or fertile land is optimum for wheat cultivation. A 50 to 100 centimeters of rainfall is required for the cultivation of wheat
    Rainfall 20-100 cm (ideal ~75 cm)
    Climate Wheat cultivation requires cool climate and medium rainfall. The ideal temperature needed in winters for the production of this crop is 10 degree to 15 degree celsius.
    Soil Type Clay loam, Sandy loam
    Irrigation Strategy Drip irrigation, with its low application rate, prevents the saturation of water within the root zone and continuously maintains field capacity.
    Insecticide and Pesticide The two main chemical families used post-emerge are the pyrethroids and the organophosphates (OP). Pyrethroids include products like Silencer, Warrior II, and Mustang Max. They generally have around a 30-day PHI (pre-harvest interval)
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen for large leaves, rapid growth and development. Phosphate to supply the energy for growth and development. Potassium for plant water regulation and structural integrity. Sulphur for yield and quality improvements.
    Life Cycle Spring wheat needs about 100–130 days to grow, while growing winter wheat takes about 180–250 days. Winter wheat growth time includes up to 90 days during the colder months when it goes dormant.
    Disease and Management Major wheat diseases are rusts, smut, tan spot, spot blotch, fusarium head blight, common root rot, septoria blotch, powdery mildew, blast, and several viral, nematode, and bacterial diseases. These diseases badly impact the yield and cause mortality of the plants.

    Cotton

    Aspect Description
    General Info Cotton is a tropical and subtropical Kharif crop. It is a fiber crop and is known as ‘White gold’. India ranks 3rd in the production of cotton worldwide. It is a dry crop but roots need timely supply of water at maturity
    Type of Crop Kharif
    Temperature 25°C to 30°C during growth
    Terrain Cotton is grown on a variety of soils ranging from well drained deep alluvial soils in the north to black clayey soils of varying depth in central region and in black and mixed black and red soils in south zone
    Rainfall 600-1200 mm annually; well-distributed
    Climate Cotton is a tropical crop and it is raised in India as a Kharif crop. Cotton requires uniformly high temperature between 21 degree Celsius and 30 degree Celsius
    Soil Type Well-drained sandy loam or clay loam; pH 5.5-7.5
    Irrigation Strategy Supplemental irrigation during dry periods; flood or drip irrigation
    Insecticide and Pesticide Integrated pest management; treatments for bollworms, aphids, and leafhoppers
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium, Sulfur
    Life Cycle Germination, seedling stage, vegetative growth, flowering, boll formation, maturation
    Disease and Management Verticillium wilt, Fusarium wilt, cotton leaf curl virus; resistant varieties, crop rotation, fungicides

    Sugarcane

    Aspect Description
    General Info Sugarcane is an important cash crop. India stands at 2nd position among all countries in the world in its production. Sugarcane crop requires a long rainy season of at least 7-8 months. Traditional Sugarcane Production was in North India but it has also shifted to South India. North India Sugarcane are of sub-tropical variety and so have low sugar content. Also sugar factories have to remain shut in winter seasons in North India. South India- Tropical Variety and coastal areas hence have high sugar content and high yield.
    Type of Crop Kharif, Rabi
    Temperature 25°C to 30°C during growth
    Terrain Sugarcane can be grown an all types of soils ranging from sandy loam to clay loam. It, however, thrives best on well drained soils.
    Rainfall 1000-1500 mm annually; well-distributed
    Climate Tropical and subtropical
    Soil Type Well-drained sandy loam or clay loam; pH 5.5-8.0
    Irrigation Strategy Regular irrigation; flood or drip irrigation systems
    Insecticide and Pesticide Integrated pest management; treatments for borers, scales, and leafhoppers
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium, Sulfur
    Life Cycle Planting, tillering, grand growth, maturation, harvest
    Disease and Management Smuts, leaf scald, red rot; resistant varieties, crop rotation, fungicides

    Tea

    Aspect Description
    General Info Tea is an evergreen plant that mainly grows in tropical and subtropical climates. Tea is a labour intensive crop and 50% of the labourers are women. It grows faster under light shade. Commercial cultivation of tea started in India from the British era. India is the 2nd largest producer and the largest consumer of tea in the world. Tea plants require high rainfall but its roots cannot tolerate water logging. Hence, it requires sloppy areas
    Type of Crop Plantation Crops
    Temperature 15°C to 30°C during growth
    Terrain :A mountainous terrain is often ideal. The tea tree grows best in a tropical and subtropical climate
    Rainfall 1200-2500 mm annually; evenly distributed
    Climate tea is a perennial plant that grows well in both tropical and subtropical regions. It requires a moderately hot and humid climate
    Soil Type Acidic to neutral, well-drained loamy soil; pH 4.5-5.8
    Irrigation Strategy drip irrigation can reduce soil compaction, reduce surface runoff, prevent the loss of topsoil and fertilizers, and because drip irrigation water seeps into soil capillaries, the soil is deep and wide, which is conducive to the absorption of tea roots
    Insecticide and Pesticide Integrated pest management; treatments for tea mosquito, red spider mite, and thrips
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium, Magnesium
    Life Cycle Germination, establishment, vegetative growth, flowering, harvesting (plucking)
    Disease and Management Tea blister blight, grey blight, root rot; resistant varieties, proper sanitation, fungicides

    Finger Millet

    Aspect Description
    General Info Finger millet, also known as Ragi, is a robust and highly nutritious cereal that is predominantly cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions. It is known for its high calcium content and other essential minerals, making it a vital part of the diet in many developing countries. India is one of the largest producers of finger millet.
    Type of Crop Kharif
    Temperature 20°C to 30°C during growth
    Terrain Finger millet thrives in sandy to sandy-loam soils, and can tolerate a range of soil types including less fertile and rocky soils.
    Rainfall Minimum 500 mm annually; drought-resistant but prefers well-distributed rainfall
    Climate Tropical and subtropical
    Soil Type Well-drained, fertile loamy to sandy-loam soil
    Irrigation Strategy Requires minimal irrigation; rainfed cultivation is common, but supplemental irrigation can enhance yields
    Insecticide and Pesticide Neem oil, Bacillus thuringiensis for eco-friendly pest management; chemical pesticides like Chlorpyrifos may be used in severe cases
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium
    Life Cycle Germination, vegetative growth, flowering, grain filling, maturation
    Disease and Management Blast, foot rot, smut; use of disease-resistant varieties, crop rotation, and fungicides

    Maize

    Aspect Description
    General Info Maize, also known as corn, is one of the most widely cultivated crops in the world, serving as a staple food, livestock feed, and a raw material for industry. It is highly versatile and can be grown in a variety of climates and soils. The United States is the largest producer of maize.
    Type of Crop Kharif, Rabi
    Temperature 21°C to 30°C during growth
    Terrain Maize grows best in deep, well-drained soils with good organic matter and a pH of 5.5 to 7.5.
    Rainfall 500 mm to 800 mm annually; requires well-distributed rainfall or irrigation
    Climate Tropical, subtropical, and temperate
    Soil Type Well-drained, fertile loamy soil
    Irrigation Strategy Requires regular irrigation, especially during the flowering and grain filling stages; drip irrigation and sprinkler systems are effective
    Insecticide and Pesticide Integrated Pest Management (IPM) practices; common pesticides include lambda-cyhalothrin and imidacloprid
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium
    Life Cycle Germination, vegetative growth, tasseling, silking, grain filling, maturation
    Disease and Management Downy mildew, leaf blight, rust; use of resistant varieties, crop rotation, and fungicides

    Black Gram

    Aspect Description
    General Info Black gram, also known as urad dal, is a highly valued pulse crop in India. It is an important source of protein and is used in various culinary dishes. Black gram is well-suited to dryland farming and is often grown as a catch crop or in crop rotation systems.
    Type of Crop Kharif, Rabi
    Temperature 25°C to 35°C during growth
    Terrain Black gram can be grown in a variety of soils, but it thrives best in loamy to sandy-loam soils with good drainage.
    Rainfall 600 mm to 800 mm annually; requires well-distributed rainfall
    Climate Tropical and subtropical
    Soil Type Well-drained, loamy to sandy-loam soil
    Irrigation Strategy Requires minimal irrigation; supplemental irrigation during dry spells can enhance yield
    Insecticide and Pesticide Neem oil, Bacillus thuringiensis for eco-friendly pest management; chemical pesticides like imidacloprid may be used in severe cases
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium
    Life Cycle Germination, vegetative growth, flowering, pod formation, maturation
    Disease and Management Yellow mosaic virus, leaf curl, powdery mildew; use of disease-resistant varieties, crop rotation, and fungicides

    Ground Nut

    Aspect Description
    General Info Groundnut, also known as peanut, is a significant oilseed crop and a valuable source of protein. It is grown in tropical and subtropical regions and is used for oil extraction, as a food crop, and for fodder. India, China, and the United States are among the largest producers of groundnut.
    Type of Crop Kharif, Rabi
    Temperature 20°C to 30°C during growth
    Terrain Groundnut grows best in well-drained sandy loam or loamy soils with good organic matter content and a pH of 6.0 to 6.5.
    Rainfall 500 mm to 1000 mm annually; requires well-distributed rainfall or supplemental irrigation
    Climate Tropical and subtropical
    Soil Type Well-drained, sandy loam to loamy soil
    Irrigation Strategy Requires regular irrigation, especially during flowering and pod development stages; drip irrigation and sprinkler systems are effective
    Insecticide and Pesticide Integrated Pest Management (IPM) practices; common pesticides include Chlorpyrifos and Mancozeb
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium, Calcium, Magnesium
    Life Cycle Germination, vegetative growth, flowering, pegging, pod development, maturation
    Disease and Management Leaf spot, rust, stem rot; use of disease-resistant varieties, crop rotation, and fungicides

    Oats

    Aspect Description
    General Info Oats are a popular cereal grain known for their nutritional benefits and versatility. They are widely used for human consumption as oatmeal and rolled oats, as well as for livestock feed. Major producers of oats include Russia, Canada, and the United States.
    Type of Crop Rabi
    Temperature 15°C to 25°C during growth
    Terrain Oats thrive in well-drained loamy soils with high organic matter. They are tolerant of poor soil conditions compared to other cereal crops.
    Rainfall 400 mm to 800 mm annually; prefers well-distributed rainfall
    Climate Temperate
    Soil Type Well-drained, fertile loamy to sandy-loam soil
    Irrigation Strategy Oats generally require minimal irrigation; however, supplemental irrigation during dry periods can improve yield
    Insecticide and Pesticide Integrated Pest Management (IPM) practices; common pesticides include Chlorpyrifos and Imidacloprid
    Essential Nutrients Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium
    Life Cycle Germination, vegetative growth, tillering, heading, grain filling, maturation
    Disease and Management Crown rust, stem rust, leaf blotch; use of disease-resistant varieties, crop rotation, and fungicides
